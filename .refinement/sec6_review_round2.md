## Minor Issues (should fix)

1. **Cross-section terminology: "persistence forecast" vs. "persistence baseline."** Section 3.3 introduces $v^{\text{persist}}_t$ as the "persistence baseline" (line 69 of sec3_formulation.md). Section 6 consistently calls it the "persistence forecast." Both are acceptable on their own, but having two terms for the same quantity across the paper is a source of reviewer confusion. One of the two sections should be adjusted so that the same term appears throughout. Given that Section 3 is already finalized, the path of least resistance is to change Section 6's two occurrences of "persistence forecast" to "persistence baseline" to match Section 3.

2. **Herding classification: the $\Delta_i > 0.003$ threshold is stated but not motivated.** The anchored threshold (0.002) is motivated as "numerical noise." The overconfidence threshold pair (0.85 confidence, 0.005 revision) is motivated as "amplifying its prior." The herding threshold of 0.003 is stated without explanation of why it differs from the anchored threshold. A brief parenthetical, such as "above the anchored threshold to ensure the revision reflects substantive movement," would close this gap.

3. **Section 6.4: the phrase "By construction" for the Myerson-Shapley gap sign.** The claim that $\mu_i(t) - \phi_i(t) > 0$ for network-dependent agents is stated with "By construction." This is correct in the cooperative game theory sense but may not be immediately obvious to a forecasting audience. One additional clause, such as "because Myerson restricts the coalitions in which agent $a_i$ receives credit to those where it is connected, agents that contribute only through network position see their Myerson value diverge upward from their Shapley value," would make the logic self-contained.

4. **The code applies `max(confidence, 0.01)` as a weight floor (line 428 of debate_system.py).** The paper says "weights renormalised within the active coalition" and $c_i(t) \in (0,1]$ "strictly positive by prompt design" (Section 3.3). These are not contradictory (the code floor is a safety net for parsing failures), but a footnote acknowledging this numerical smoothing would prevent a reviewer who reads the code from flagging an inconsistency. This was noted in Round 1 (m8) but was not among the tracked issues; it remains a low-priority item.

5. **The edge weight formula (7) uses a source-round notation that could be made more explicit.** The formula references $\hat{v}_i^{(1)}(t)$ for the source agent, meaning the source's Round 1 position. Since the edge captures the Round 1 to Round 2 transition, the superscript (1) is correct, but a reader might momentarily wonder whether the source's Round 2 output is used anywhere in the construction. A brief parenthetical like "where $\hat{v}_i^{(1)}(t)$ is the source agent's Round 1 position, since only Round 1 outputs are visible during the revision" would eliminate this ambiguity. This is very minor.
